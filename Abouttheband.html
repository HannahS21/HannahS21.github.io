Twenty One Pilots is a band that was founded by the lead singer, Tyler Joseph in 2009 from Columbus, Ohio. The band originally had 3 members; Tyler Joseph, Nick Thomas, and Chris Salih. Nick Thomas and Chris Salih both left the band in 2011 and were both replaced by Josh Dun. As of 2020, Tyler Joseph and Josh Dun are the only members of Twenty One Pilots. Josh Dun is the drummer of the band and Tyler Joseph is the Singer.

The band first got recognition in 2015 for their single, "Stressed Out" on their album, Blurryface. The band won a grammy in 2017 at the 59th Annual Grammy Awards for the song. The band released 2 albums before being signed to Fueled by Ramen in 2012. The band has put out 5 albums as of 2020. In 2016, the band had the oppurtunity to make a song for the movie "Suicide Squad".

Both members are Christian, however, they do not refer to themselves as a Christian band. They don't have a specific genre to their name because their music is so different and a combination of many things like rap, pop, rock, and many more. As of 2020 the band has been nominated for 103 awards and have won 32. The band has also preformed at many festivals including Music Midtown, Reading Festival, Firefly Festival, and more. They have also preformed on many award shows and even "Saturday Night Live".
